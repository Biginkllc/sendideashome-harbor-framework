## Founding Backstory (Origin Narrative)
I am constantly thinking of ways to send my IFAMs to developing countries like Burkina Faso. I've had major universities in the country and administrators in mind, and I’ve thought about how I'm not the only person possessing the ingrained spirit of **Afroingenuity** and innovation—willing to forego the capitalistic nature of the country of which I now preside—to make my contribution to the land of my ancestors.

This project is framed as a **Harbor for Ideas**, widening the path for both inbound seekers and outbound intellectual contributions. It aims to bypass the high cost of patenting through **time-stamping / hashing of ideas**, while preserving the contributor as the **Originator / Giver** as a lifetime benefit.

## What This Is (Plain Language)
**SendIdeasHome** is a Harbor for Ideas.

Not a marketplace.  
Not a patent office.  
Not a gate.  

A Harbor:
- helps ideas **arrive safely**
- helps ideas **depart freely**
- keeps a **logbook**
- keeps **names attached**
- keeps a **time record** of what happened and when

## The Harbor Logic (Parable)
A Harbor does not own the ships.  
It serves them.

People arrive with ideas the way ships arrive with cargo.  
The Harbor’s job is to:
1) **receive**
2) **record**
3) **protect**
4) **send onward**
5) **remember the sender**

In this system:
- the “cargo” is an idea (an IFAM)
- the “logbook” is the proof record (timestamp + hash)
- the “flag” on the ship is authorship attribution
- the “departure” is gifting the idea outward for use and improvement

---

## Proof of Origin (Timestamping / Hashing)
This project uses the low-cost logic of “time anchoring”:
- record the idea (text, image, audio, repo commit, or PDF)
- generate a hash for that content
- store the record in at least two places (“two-is-one” redundancy):
  - GitHub repository (human-readable)
  - Web3/IPFS or similar timestamp reference (proof trail)

Goal:
- bypass expensive patent gates
- preserve authorship and origin attribution
- keep the giver named as the originator

> The point is not to “own” the idea.  
> The point is to keep the giver from being erased.

---

## Giver Recognition (Lifetime Benefit)
In the SendIdeasHome ethic:
- A gift does not mean disappearance.
- A gift becomes a trail.

Every submission is designed to preserve:
- Originator/Giver name or chosen pseudonym
- Date/time of the record
- A stable reference to the original artifact
- A simple “permission posture” (gifted for use, with attribution)

This becomes a lifetime benefit because the Harbor remembers the giver.
